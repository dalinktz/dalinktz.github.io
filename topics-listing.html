Diseases Management

Fish diseases may cause severe losses on fish farms through:reduced fish growth and production, increased feeding cost caused by lack of appetite and waste of uneaten feed, increased vulnerability to predation, increased susceptibility to low water quality and death of fish.

Trending Topics

Rise of sustainable practices

This includes things like using alternative feed sources to reduce reliance on wild fish, developing closed-loop systems that minimize waste and pollution, and breeding fish that are more resistant to disease. These practices are becoming increasingly important as consumers become more concerned about the environmental impact of their seafood choices.
Cellular Agriculture

This emerging technology involves growing fish meat in controlled conditions from fish cells, without the need for raising live fish. While still in its early stages, cellular agriculture has the potential to revolutionize the seafood industry by reducing its environmental footprint and meeting the growing demand for seafood.
