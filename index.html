Fish farming

Fish farming is a form of aquaculture in which fish are raised in enclosures to be sold as food. It is the fastest growing area of animal food production. Today, about half the fish consumed globally are raised in these artificial environments. Commonly farmed species include salmon, tuna, cod, trout and halibut. These “aquafarms” can take the form of mesh cages submerged in natural bodies of water, or concrete enclosures on land.

How does fish farming work

The fish farming industry has a variety of systems in place to increase production. The methods used depend on the species of fish being raised and the location of the farm.

Fish farms are usually located inside small bodies of water, such as lakes or ponds, or in the sea along the coast.

Cage aquaculture

This method involves raising fish in mesh cages suspended in lakes, rivers, or oceans. It's suitable for commercially important fish species that thrive in open water environments.

Pond aquaculture

This type of fish farming involves raising fish in ponds or tanks. It's often used for freshwater fish species and can be intensive or extensive depending on the stocking density and level of management.

Integrated aquaculture

This method combines fish farming with another agricultural activity, such as crop farming or livestock raising. The waste from one system can be used as fertilizer for the other, creating a more sustainable system.

Recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS)

These are closed-loop systems that treat and reuse water, making them suitable for land-based fish farming. RAS can be used for both freshwater and saltwater fish species.

Tilapia fish

This freshwater fish is popular for its adaptability to various environments. It has a fast growth rate, is tolerant of changes in water quality, and hasa mild, flaky flesh that appeals to a wide range of consumers.

Catfish

Catfish are another popular choice for aquaculture due to their hardiness and ability to thrive in ponds or tanks. They have a high feed conversion rate, meaning they efficiently convert feed into edible fish, and their mild-flavored white flesh is in high demand.

Salmon

Salmon are a cold-water fish that can be raised in freshwater or saltwater environments. They have a high market value due to their rich flavor and omega-3 fatty acids. However, salmon require more specific water quality conditions and are more susceptible to disease than tilapia or catfish.

Natural food

is found naturally in the pond. It may include detritus, bacteria, plankton, worms, insects, snails, aquatic plants and fish. Their abundance greatly depends on water quality.in particular organic fertilization, can help you to provide a good supply of natural food to your fish.

Complete feed

They are made from a mixture of carefully selected ingredients to provide all the nutrients necessary for the fish to grow well. They must be made in a form which the fish find easy to eat and digest. These feeds are quite difficult to make on the farm and are usually quite expensive to buy.

Supplementary feeds

are feeds regularly distributed to the fish in the pond. They usually consist of cheap materials locally available such as terrestrial plants, kitchen wastes or agricultural by-products.

Frequently Asked Questions

There are several fish farming systems, each with advantages and disadvantages. These include cage culture, pond culture, recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS), and integrated aquaculture.

Maintaining Water quality, preventing diseases, and ensuring a supply of high-quality fish feed are some of the challenges faced by fish farmers.

Salmon, tilapia, carp, catfish, and shrimp are some of the most popular choices for fish farming globally. The selection depends on factors like climate, market demand, and farming experience.
